London Stolen Animals Project

Category: London Crimes

This project analysed Metropolitan Police data from 2010 to 2023 to identify patterns in animal thefts, highlighting trends in stolen animal types, theft hotspots, recovery rates, and providing recommendations to improve prevention and recovery strategies.
Key findings include:

  • - Rising Theft Rates: Animal thefts have increased, with a significant spike in 2022 due to the theft of 1,206 fish in Barking and Dagenham.
  • - Theft Hotspots: Boroughs like Barking and Dagenham, Croydon, and Lambeth report the highest theft rates, often due to high pet ownership and poor security.
  • - Commonly Stolen Animals: Dogs are the most frequently stolen (82%), followed by fish and birds.
  • - Recovery Rates: Only 6.15% of stolen animals are recovered, with dogs having the highest recovery rate at 12%.

Recommendations: Focus on awareness campaigns in high-risk areas, increase microchipping, improve home security, and strengthen community partnerships to prevent and recover stolen animals.

London Homicide Project

Category: London Crimes

This project analysed homicide data from 2003 to 2023, identifying trends in offence types, weapon usage, victim demographics, geographic hotspots, and case resolutions to inform crime prevention and law enforcement strategies.
Key findings include:

  • - Declining Homicide Rates: Homicides decreased overall, with spikes in 2017 and 2019.
  • - Weapon Trends: Knives or sharp implements were involved in 52% of homicides, highlighting ongoing knife crime issues.
  • - Victim Demographics: Males (76%) and the 25-35 age group are most affected by homicide.
  • - Geographic Hotspots: Boroughs like Barking and Dagenham, Croydon, and Bromley show high homicide rates, linked to socio-economic factors and gang activity.
  • - Case Resolution: 94% of homicide cases have been solved, with 6% remaining unsolved.

Recommendations: include focusing on knife crime and domestic violence awareness, engaging young males in violence prevention programs, increasing law enforcement in high-risk areas, and enhancing cold case investigations with advanced forensics.

Coffee Shop Sales Project

Category: Coffee Sales

This project analysed transaction data from three NYC coffee shop locations to uncover customer purchasing behaviour, product performance, and store-specific trends to optimise inventory, marketing strategies, and revenue.
Key findings include:

  • - Sales Trends: Overall sales have steadily increased, with weekdays (especially Monday, Thursday, and Friday mornings) outperforming weekends.
  • - Product Performance: Coffee (39%) leads sales, with Ethiopian and Brazilian coffees being the most popular. Espresso shots and dark chocolate underperform.
  • - Store-Specific Insights: Astrovia favours Colombian coffee, Hell’s Kitchen prefers Brazilian coffee, and Lower Manhattan has high demand for Ethiopian coffee.

Recommendations: Optimise staffing during peak hours, promote low-performing items, customise offerings per store, boost weekend sales with promotions, and maximise revenue from bestsellers like Jamaican Coffee River and Morning Sunrise Chai.

CityMart Sales Project

Category: Retail Sales

This project analysed retail sales data from 2023, focusing on Beauty, Clothing, and Electronics to identify trends, customer preferences, and opportunities for inventory optimisation and marketing strategies.
Key findings include:

  • - Peak Sales: Occurred in May and October, driven by Electronics and Beauty products.
  • - Gender Insights: Females dominated Beauty and Clothing, while males spent more on Electronics.
  • - Age Insights: The 18-23 age group favoured Beauty and Clothing, while the 60-64 group preferred Electronics.
  • - Product Performance: Beauty, Clothing, and Electronics had distinct seasonal peaks, with high-value items performing well in May and December.

Recommendations: Target campaigns during peak months, focus on gender-specific marketing, engage age groups with tailored campaigns, and optimise inventory based on seasonal demand.

Fire Incidents During Major Events Project

Category: Fire Incidents

This project analysed fire data in London from 2009 to 2024, identifying trends, high-risk boroughs, and ignition sources during events like Diwali, Halloween, Guy Fawkes, and Christmas, with recommendations to enhance fire safety strategies.
Key findings include:

  • - Bonfire Night accounts for the highest proportion of fire incidents (51%).
  • - Tower Hamlets, Westminster, and Southwark are the top boroughs with the most incidents.
  • - Dwellings (32%) and outdoor structures (25%) are the most affected property types.

Recommendations focus on targeted awareness campaigns, stricter firework regulations, and seasonal resource deployment to reduce incidents during peak periods.

Public Transport Journeys Project

Category: Transport for London (TfL)

This project analysed transport journey data in London from 2010 to 2024 to uncover trends, identify peak and low periods, and provide recommendations for improving transport infrastructure and services.
Key findings include:

  • - Declining Trends: Bus and Underground journeys have declined in recent years, particularly since 2019, likely due to the pandemic.
  • - Peak and Low Periods: September and July see the highest transport usage, while October shows a significant dip.
  • - Mode Contribution: Buses dominate with 52.49% of total journeys in 2023/24, followed by the Underground (33.15%).
  • - Yearly Trends: The highest bus journeys occurred in 2015/16 (203.4 million), and the lowest in 2021/22 (95.9 million). Underground journeys peaked in 2019/20.
  • - Growth in Niche Modes: Other modes like DLR, Tram, and London Cable Car show steady growth, with specific peaks during certain years.

Recommendations: Expand bus and underground services, optimise transport for peak months, invest in TfL Rail and Overground for outer zones, and adjust services based on seasonal demand.
